Buy a New-Build Apartment in Bucharest, No Middleman

Apartments direct from the owners

Comparing Bucharest with other European capitals and looking for a new-build apartment you can buy without an agent standing in the middle, and without a buyer's commission on top? Below is the full picture: what “new-build” actually means here, how buying direct from the owner works, and a concrete listing in the Titan-Dristor area: Ilioara Residence.

What a new-build apartment, direct from the owners, actually means

A new-build apartment is one that went up recently, with today's wiring, structure and finishes, not a decades-old building patched up along the way. You are the first person to live in it. Nobody used it before you, and nothing has been quietly repaired underneath the surface.

Direct from the owners means you deal with the people actually selling, not a chain of intermediaries. The listed price is the price. It is not a starting point that grows once other parties get added along the way.

  • Recent construction, finished or nearly finished, with a warranty from the builder.
  • The listed price is the final price, always quoted plus TVA (Romania's VAT, added at invoicing).
  • You talk directly with the owners, with no messages relayed through a third party.

No buyer's commission: what it means for your money

Buy through a classic agency and a buyer's commission shows up somewhere in the deal. Sometimes it is a separate line in the contract, sometimes it is already folded into the price. On a Bucharest apartment, that percentage adds up to thousands of euros.

Buy direct from the owners and the buyer's commission disappears from the equation. That money stays with you, or goes into the apartment itself: better finishes, a parking spot, furniture. It does not go to a middleman.

That is a concrete difference, not a slogan, and it holds regardless of which country you are buying from. Fewer people between you and the apartment means fewer costs stacked onto the price, and direct answers to your questions.

New build or older building: how to choose

An older apartment has one real advantage: it already exists, you can see it, move in fast, and it is often closer to the centre. That is why it stays in demand. But it comes with tired wiring, weak insulation and bills to match.

A new build wins on comfort and monthly costs: insulated walls, windows that keep the cold out, efficient heating. You pay a bit more upfront, but you save every winter, year after year, which matters even more once you compare it with running costs back home.

There is no universal rule. It depends on your budget, how long you plan to keep the apartment, and how much a new, quiet building matters to you compared with a central location.

  • Older: available now, central, but old wiring and higher bills.
  • New: thermal comfort, low monthly costs, you are the first occupant, warranty included.
  • The decision comes down to budget, area and how many years you plan to stay.

What a new apartment costs, and what matters besides the price

The price per square metre does not tell the whole story on its own. Two apartments at the same listed price can mean very different things, depending on insulation, finishes and area. A cheaper square metre in a poorly insulated building ends up costing you more long-term, through the bills.

Market figures shift from one month to the next, so any number you read online should be checked against the actual current listing, not an average pulled from a search. Our prices are transparent, come straight from the owners, and are always quoted plus TVA. You will find them next to every apartment, with no surprises.

Beyond the price, look at what you actually get: year of construction, structure, finishes, features and location. That is where you see whether you are paying for a name or for a building that holds up.

How to find a new apartment direct from the owner, with no agency

Look for new buildings where the person who built it is the one selling, not a string of reposted listings. The good sign: one listed price, clear floor plans, one person you can actually talk to.

Go see it in person, even if that means arranging the viewing from abroad ahead of time. Look at the building, the street, the floor, the real state of construction. Ask the uncomfortable questions too: paperwork, deadlines, warranty. A serious seller does not dodge them, wherever you are calling from.

Ask for the paperwork at the viewing and check the construction stage yourself, with your own eyes. Do that regardless of who you are buying from, an agency or the owner directly.

Frequently asked questions

What does it mean to buy an apartment direct from the owner, with no commission?
It means you negotiate and sign with whoever is actually selling, with no intermediary in between. The listed price is the final price, and there is no buyer's commission. The money you would have paid in commission stays with you.
How much do you save by buying without a real estate agency?
A buyer's commission through a classic agency adds up, on a Bucharest apartment, to thousands of euros. When you buy direct from the owners, that amount stays with you, money that can go toward furnishing the place or a parking spot.
Which is better, a new apartment or an older one in Bucharest?
It depends on your budget and how long you plan to keep it. Older apartments are available now and often more central. New builds win on insulation, thermal comfort and lower monthly bills. For anyone planning to stay for years, the new build pays for itself through savings on maintenance.
How do you find a new apartment direct from the owner, with no intermediary?
Look for new buildings where the person who built it is selling directly, with one listed price and one person you deal with. Go see it in person, check the construction stage, and ask for the paperwork. A serious seller answers every question.
How much does a new apartment cost per square metre in Bucharest?
It varies a lot from one sector to another and from month to month, so any figure needs to be checked against the actual listing, not an average found online. Our prices, quoted plus TVA, sit next to every apartment on the prices page.
What should you check before buying a new apartment?
Ask for the paperwork at the viewing and check the real construction stage yourself. Ask about the completion deadline, the warranty and the features included. See the building, the street and the floor with your own eyes, whether you are buying through an agency or direct from the owner.
