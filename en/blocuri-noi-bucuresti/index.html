Apartments direct from the owners

What to check on a new building before you pay

A beautifully finished apartment can sit inside a badly built building. Photos never show that. So the first question is not what the room looks like, but how solid the building holding it actually is.

Most project pages sell you the light and the kitchen. Very few tell you what the structure is made of, what seismic resistance it has, or what stage the construction is at right now. Those are exactly the things that matter twenty years from now, once the finishes have changed anyway.

Below are the points to ask any seller of a new building, wherever you are buying from. If one dodges them, you already have your answer.

  • Load-bearing structure: what the building is made of and how it behaves in an earthquake.
  • Materials: walls, insulation, windows, elevator.
  • Construction stage: what phase it is at now and when it will be finished.
  • Documents: building permit, completion certificate, structural warranty.
  • Who is selling: the owner directly, or a middleman.

The structure: what the building is made of, and why it matters

The load-bearing structure is the building's skeleton, the part that carries the weight and stands up to an earthquake. In new Bucharest buildings you mainly see it in two forms: reinforced concrete frames (columns and beams that form a kind of cage) or shear walls, also called diaphragms (solid concrete walls that absorb the forces). Many buildings combine both.

For an ordinary buyer, the simple idea is this: what matters is that the structure is properly poured reinforced concrete, not masonry stacked on masonry. Interior partition walls can be lighter, but the skeleton needs to be concrete.

At Ilioara Residence the structure is reinforced concrete plus BCA Ytong masonry (a lightweight, well-insulating block), designed for high seismic resistance. The facade is insulated with 10 cm of polystyrene and a water-resistant render, and the elevator is a Schindler, one of the established brands in the market.

Earthquake resistance, explained simply

Bucharest sits in a seismic zone, so the question is not whether the building will hold, but how well it was designed for it. This is the big difference between an old building and a new one: new buildings are built to today's seismic design codes, considerably stricter than those from a few decades ago, the same general direction building codes have moved in across Europe.

Older buildings in the city are classified by seismic risk class, from the most serious downward. A new building, put up today under current codes, starts from a completely different position: it is designed to behave in a controlled way in an earthquake, not just to avoid collapse.

What you can concretely ask for: what the structure is made of, whether the project follows current seismic codes, and to see the poured concrete on site with your own eyes. On a building still under construction, that is something you can genuinely verify, unlike one that is already plastered over.

Construction stage: shell, grey, white, turnkey

When you buy into a building that is not finished yet, you will hear these terms. They are simple: shell stage means the structure and walls are up, no finishes. Grey stage means the wiring and base plastering are in. White stage means it is ready for fine finishes. Turnkey means you move in and that is it, nothing left to touch.

The earlier you buy, the lower the price per square metre, because you are accepting that you wait. The closer it is to finished, the more you pay, but you see exactly what you are getting. It is a trade-off between price and patience, and everyone makes it differently depending on where they are buying from and how soon they need the keys.

Ilioara Residence is under construction now, with completion set for December 2026. The advantage: you lock in today's price for a home you will get turnkey in a few months, not in three years. And because the building is going up on site, you can come see the real stage of construction yourself, not a rendering, even if that means planning one trip specifically for it.

The documents to ask for, without hesitation

A serious seller does not mind when you ask for the paperwork. The one who gets uneasy is the one with nothing to show. These are the papers that matter on a new building:

Beyond the documents, there is something few sellers volunteer on their own: under Romanian law, the Construction Quality Law (Law 10/1995) requires a 10-year warranty on a building's load-bearing structure. That means if a defect appears in the building's skeleton within the first ten years, the builder is legally responsible, regardless of where the buyer is from. It is one of the strongest protections you get, and almost nobody puts it on the table unprompted.

  • The building permit, which shows the building is going up legally.
  • The completion acceptance certificate, the sign that the work was inspected and approved.
  • The building's technical book, the file with everything used and how it was built.
  • The legal structural warranty, under Law 10/1995.

Why buying direct from the owners matters

Here is the second half of the question. It is not just what you buy that matters, it is who from. Buy through a chain of intermediaries and, somewhere along the way, a commission appears along with a game of broken telephone: you ask one thing, and something else reaches the person who actually knows the answer.

Buy direct from the owners and the buyer's commission disappears. The money goes into the apartment, not into intermediaries. And more importantly for the technical questions above, you talk directly with the people responsible for the building. You ask about structure, materials, construction stage, and you get the answer from the source, not second-hand, wherever in the world you are asking from.

Frequently asked questions

How do you check if a new building can withstand an earthquake?
You ask what the load-bearing structure is made of and whether the project follows current seismic codes. New buildings are built to standards considerably stricter than those from a few decades ago, specifically so they behave in a controlled way during an earthquake. On a building still under construction, you can even see the poured concrete on site, before it gets covered by finishes.
What is safer in a new building: reinforced concrete frames or shear walls?
Both are valid solutions, and many buildings combine them. Frames are concrete columns and beams that form a skeleton, while shear walls (diaphragms) are solid concrete walls that absorb the forces. What matters for a buyer is that the skeleton is properly poured reinforced concrete, not just masonry. You find out the specific detail by asking the seller for information about the structure.
What do shell, grey, white and turnkey stage mean for an apartment?
Shell stage means the structure and walls are up, with no finishes. Grey stage means the wiring and base plastering are done. White stage means it is ready for fine finishes. Turnkey means you move in directly, with nothing left to do. The earlier you buy, the lower the price, but the longer you wait.
What warranty do you get on the structure of a new apartment?
Romania's Construction Quality Law (Law 10/1995) provides a 10-year warranty on a building's load-bearing structure. If a defect appears in the building's skeleton within the first ten years, the builder is legally responsible. It is one of the strongest protections you get when buying a new building.
What documents should you ask for before buying a new apartment?
The building permit, the completion acceptance certificate, and the building's technical book. Plus the legal structural warranty. A serious seller shows them without hesitation. Anyone who avoids these questions is already telling you something.
What do you gain by buying direct from the owners here?
You do not pay a buyer's commission, so the money goes into the apartment, not to intermediaries. And you talk directly with the people responsible for the building, so questions about structure, materials and construction stage get answered at the source. The listed price is the final price, always with TVA stated upfront.
